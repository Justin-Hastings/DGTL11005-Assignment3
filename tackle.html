Tackle Suggestions


Bait Setups


Most anglers have a rod for bait fishing as this is a quick and easy way of fishing. If using frozen bait, it must be fresh, although most people prefer to catch their own live bait as this works best.

  • Line is usually 10-30lb
  • Only one knot is required for bait fishing, if it can be used for terminal tackle, such as the locked blood knot
  • The required terminal tackle includes swivels, sinkers and hooks
  • Knowing how to tie a few different rigs is a good idea, but you can still have fun with just tying a hook onto the end of some line

Lure Setups


Another big aspect of fishing is using lures and soft plastics. This style of fishing is different to bait fishing as you are trying to mimic a live fish with a piece of plastic.

  • Line is usually 20-30lb
  • A few knots are required for lure fishing to connect leader to mainline and tying on lures


Some Bait Rigs:

Paternoster Rig:

  • Commonly used when fishing in deeper water
  • Easy to change hooks


Running Ball Sinker Rig:

  • Lets the bait sit up off the bottom
  • Easily change the sinker to suit the water depth and current


Snelled Hook Rig:

  • Good for rigging a long strip bait
  • Can be used with a running ball sinker rig


Lure Rigs:

Lure - Tied On:

  • No possibility of a clip accidentally opening (very rare)


Lure - Clipped On:

  • Easy to swap lures
  • Doesn't shorten the leader when swapping lures



For most people fishing is the fun, catching fish is a bonus.
